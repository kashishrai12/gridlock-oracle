Here's the whole thing in plain terms.

**What we ended up doing.** We started trying to predict how long an event takes to clear, but the data couldn't support that (the timing columns were 97% empty, and the model scored near zero). So we pivoted to something the data *can* predict: **whether a new event will need a road closure.** That directly answers the "should we send barricades?" question in your theme.

**What information the model uses.** Only things known the moment an event is reported (no cheating with future info): the event cause, vehicle type, police station, junction/corridor/zone, planned-vs-unplanned, priority, and time features (hour, day, weekend). The strongest predictors turned out to be **event cause, police station, and vehicle type.**

**What the model predicts.** For each incoming event it outputs a **probability that the event will require a road closure**. From that probability we compute a simple, transparent impact score (0–10) and a recommendation: barricades, personnel count, whether to set up a diversion. The "expected clearance time" shown is *not* predicted — it's just the historical average for that junction, clearly labeled as descriptive.

**Numbers to record for your report:**
